Engineering Physics

Engineering Physics is an accredited engineering degree program offered at the University of British Columbia. Often referred to as the most difficult program on campus, students in Engineering Physics take a total of 177 credits over the course of their degree. Core courses are in Computer Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Physics, and Math. Alongside the core courses in my degree I have taken / am taking the following technical electives:

MATH 220 (Math Proofs)

CPSC 221 (Algorithms and Data Structures)
